Research Questions — Answered by Data
Q1: Does higher compensation reliably reduce attrition?
Answer: Partially.

Within the same job role, employees who left earned 30% less than those who stayed ($4,787 vs $6,833). This is strong evidence that compensation matters.

However, high earners also appear in the attrition cohort — especially when combined with OverTime=Yes or YearsSinceLastPromotion >3.

Conclusion: Compensation is necessary but not sufficient. Fix workload and growth paths first. Pay is often the final straw, not the root cause.
Q2: Is attrition evenly distributed across departments?
Answer: No — significantly different by department.

Sales: 20.6% — driven by overtime + role-level pay gaps
HR: 19.0% — driven by promotion stagnation
R&D: 13.8% — driven by early-tenure onboarding gaps

Conclusion: One-size-fits-all retention policies will fail. Each department needs its own targeted intervention strategy based on its primary driver.
